TASK 4: IMPOSE MARKDOWN/LATEX COMPATIBILITY
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The output will be converted to PDF via LaTeX. Fix these issues:

**Asterisks in names:**
Terms containing asterisks (like O*NET, C*-algebras) break bold formatting.
- Inside bold: **Validate with O\*NET:** (escape with backslash)
- Outside bold: O*NET (no escape needed)

**Special characters that need escaping in certain contexts:**
- Ampersand: & → \& (if causing issues)
- Percent: % → \% (if causing issues)
- Underscores in names: some_variable → some\_variable (if causing issues)

**Quotation marks:**
- Use straight quotes "like this"
- Avoid smart/curly quotes

**Dashes:**
- Use -- for en-dash (ranges)
  e.g. "The Second World War (1939--1945)", "Smith (1990, pp. 10--15)"
- Use --- for em-dash (breaks):
  e.g. "The bakery's significantly broad hours of operation---6 a.m. to 6 p.m.---certainly showed concern for customers’ manifold circumstances." or "Mabel the Cat was delighted with the assortment of pastries the new bakery featured, but Harry the Dog---he felt otherwise, for the bakery did not offer cheese Danishes at all."

**Common fixes:**
- Remove any raw LaTeX commands unless intentional
- Ensure no unmatched brackets or braces
- Check that bold/italic markers are properly paired

**Escape Special Characters within Text:**
- You must identify technical terms, acronyms, or proper nouns that contain reserved Markdown or LaTeX characters (`*`, `&`, `%`, `_`, `$`). You must **escape** these characters with a backslash (`\`) so they are treated as text, not code.
- For example:
- **Asterisks in names:**
  - *Rule:* Any asterisk appearing inside a word or acronym must be escaped.
  - *Examples:* Change `C*-algebras` to `C\*-algebras`; change `O*NET` to `O\*NET`.
  - *Reasoning:* This prevents the asterisks from accidentally triggering italics if multiple appear in one paragraph.
- **Ampersands:**
  - *Rule:* Always escape ampersands in text.
  - *Examples:* Change `R&D` to `R\&D`; change `Simon & Schuster` to `Simon \& Schuster`.
- **Underscores:**
  - *Rule:* Escape underscores in file names or variable names used in prose (not in math mode).
  - *Example:* Change `data_set_final` to `data\_set\_final`.
- **Percents:**
  - *Rule:* Escape percent signs when used as a symbol. (Prefer writing "percent" as per Task 3, but if the symbol is necessary, escape it).
  - *Example:* Change `100%` to `100\%`.
